Explanation with an example

                  One of the simplest sorting algorithms is the Insertion sort. It consists of n-1 passes. For pass p=2 through n, insertion sort ensures that the elements in position 1 through p are in sorted order. It makes use of the fact that elements in positions 1 through p-1 are already known to be in sorted order.
Example:
Original 34 8 64 51 32 21 positions moved
Afterp=2 8 34 64 51 32 21 1
After p=38 34 64 51 32 21 0
After p=4 8 34 64 51 32 21 1
After p=5 8 32 34 51 64 21 3
After p=2 821 32 34 51 64 4
In pass p, we move the pth element left until its correct place is found among the first p elements.
